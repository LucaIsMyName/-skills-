# Creative briefs

**Scope:** Briefing **design/comms** work—audience, message, proof, deliverables. Not the creative itself. Pair with [`campaign-planning.md`](campaign-planning.md), [`tone-of-voice-and-brand-voice.md`](../language-&-communication/tone-of-voice-and-brand-voice.md), and [`project-briefs.md`](project-briefs.md).

## Excerpt

- **Audience** + **insight**—what they believe today vs what we need them to feel/know.
- **Single-minded proposition**—one main idea.
- **Proof points**—facts, stories (with consent).
- **Deliverables** and **formats** explicit (social, print, web).
- **Mandatories** (legal lines, disclaimers, brand assets).

## Before briefing creatives

### Concrete

- **Budget** and **timeline**; **approval** path.
- **References** (style, not copy).

### Meta

- “I’ll know it when I see it” is **not** a brief.

---

## Purpose

Give creatives **constraints that liberate**—clarity beats blank slates.

---

## 1. Bad vs good

### Bad

```text
Make it pop. Be bold.
```

### Good

```text
Audience: local donors 45–65, cautious about overhead.
Objective: explain new evening sessions; increase volunteer signups.
Proof: 3 sessions/week; quote from volunteer [approved].
Deliverables: 1080×1080 (3), story (1), landing copy.
```

## 2. What not to do

- **Change** the objective after first review without **resetting** scope.

---

## Core idea

A creative brief answers **who**, **why**, **what truth we can show**, and **what ships**.
