# Newsletter and email

**Scope:** Applies to **marketing and transactional emails** (newsletters, appeals, updates); not individual HR or legal notices, and **not** inventing unsubscribe or privacy policy text—point to legal review.

## Excerpt
- Use for **subject lines, preview text, body structure**, and a **single primary CTA** per email when possible.
- **Never invent** legal footers, consent language, or region-specific rules—**flag for legal/comms review**.
- **Ask** for list segment, send date, link targets, and brand voice before drafting.
- Full file below: rules, Bad/Good examples, and checklist.

## Purpose

This document explains how to draft **effective, trustworthy email** for **social-purpose and nonprofit organizations**: readable on mobile, honest, and aligned with supporter expectations.

## completeness before drafting

### Concrete

- **Audience segment** (donors, volunteers, service users, general)
- **One primary goal** (donate, register, read article, attend event)
- **Subject + preheader** constraints (character limits if any)
- **Links** (donation page, event, blog) — exact URLs if provided
- **Sender name** and **reply-to** if specified
- **Legal region** (e.g. UK, EU, US) for footer and consent—**do not fabricate**

### Meta

- Tone (urgent vs steady, personal vs institutional)
- **Sensitivity** (avoid guilt-tripping; respect dignity)
- Relationship to other sends (avoid duplicate ask same week—ask user)

---

## Core rules

### 1. Subject line: specific and honest

**Subject line and email body must match**—same topic; no bait-and-switch from subject to body.

### Bad: core rules

```text
You NEED to see this!!!!!!!!!!
```

### Good: core rules

```text
Open day: free debt advice this Saturday (book a slot)
```

---

### 2. Preview text supports the subject

It should **extend** the promise, not repeat the subject word-for-word.

### Bad: core rules

```text
[Preview:] Read our newsletter Read our newsletter
```

### Good: core rules

```text
[Preview:] Book a 20-minute slot with our advisers—walk-ins welcome.
```

---

### 3. One primary CTA

Multiple competing buttons reduce action. If two asks are needed, **prioritize** one visually.

### Bad: core rules

```text
[Donate now] [Volunteer] [Read 5 articles] [Survey] [Share] — all same size
```

### Good: core rules

```text
Primary: [Register for the open day]
Secondary link in text: Donate to keep advice free.
```

---

### 4. Legal footers and unsubscribe

- **Do not** write final GDPR/CAN-SPAM text unless the user pastes approved boilerplate.
- Add placeholder: `[Legal footer and unsubscribe link — insert approved text]` and recommend **legal review** for new regions.

---

### 5. Segmentation hints

When the user describes segments, **mirror** language to each group (donors vs volunteers vs beneficiaries) without conflating them in one draft unless intended.

---

## Checklist before send

- [ ] Subject + preview honest and specific
- [ ] One primary CTA clear; links tested
- [ ] Dignity-respecting tone; no manipulative guilt framing
- [ ] Footer: approved legal/unsubscribe text or explicit placeholder
- [ ] Sensitive topics signed off by comms lead if required

---

## Core idea
 Email builds **trust over time**. Clarity beats cleverness; **legal text is not optional**—get the approved block from your organization.
