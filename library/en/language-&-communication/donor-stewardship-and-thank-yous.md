# Donor stewardship and thank-yous

**Scope:** **Relationship** communications with donors—thank-yous, updates, recognition—without manipulation. Not tax/receipt law. Pair with [`fundraising-appeals.md`](fundraising-appeals.md), [`newsletter-and-email.md`](newsletter-and-email.md), [`respectful-language.md`](respectful-language.md), and [`content-design-and-microcopy.md`](../design/content-design-and-microcopy.md).

## Excerpt

- **Thank fast**—ideally within days; **specific** about what the gift enables.
- **Tone**: gratitude and **dignity**—not guilt for not giving more.
- **Segment** messages where possible (first gift vs recurring vs major).
- **Privacy**: follow preferences; **no** public naming without consent.
- **Truth**: impact claims **evidence-backed**.

## Before sending thanks

### Concrete

- **Channel** (email, post, call); **data** you may use (name, amount tier).
- **Consent** for recognition and **photography**.

### Meta

- Stewardship is **long-term**—every touch reinforces **trust** or erodes it.

---

## Purpose

Make donors feel **seen** and **informed**—so support continues for the **right reasons**.

---

## 1. Thank-you content

### Bad

```text
Thanks!!!
```

### Good

```text
Thank you, Alex. Your gift helps us run three community sessions this month. We’ll send a short update in June.
```

## 2. Recognition

- **Opt-in** for public lists; **accurate** names; easy **opt-out**.

## 3. What not to do

- **Pressure** disguised as gratitude.

---

## Core idea

Stewardship is **respect in action**: **prompt** thanks, **honest** impact, **bounded** asks.
