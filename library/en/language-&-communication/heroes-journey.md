## Purpose

This document outlines the **Hero’s Journey** as a narrative pattern. It is a **template**, not a moral verdict on who is a “hero.”

The Hero’s Journey is a classic narrative pattern with **12 stages**. It describes how a figure changes through challenges.

---

## 1. Ordinary world

The hero lives in their everyday environment.

## 2. Call to adventure

A problem or task appears.

## 3. Refusal

The hero hesitates or says no at first.

## 4. Meeting the mentor

A mentor or event encourages them to set out.

## 5. Crossing the threshold

The hero leaves the familiar world.

## 6. Tests, allies, and enemies

They face obstacles, opponents, and helpers.

## 7. Approach to the inmost cave

The conflict deepens; the hero confronts their greatest challenge.

## 8. Ordeal

The decisive trial or turning point.

## 9. Reward

The hero gains something (e.g. insight, skill, resolution).

## 10. The road back

They return, often facing further challenges.

## 11. Resurrection

The hero is transformed and matured.

## 12. Return with the elixir

They bring what they learned back and restore balance.

---

## Core idea

👉 A strong story shows **how someone moves through difficulty and changes**—without flattening real people into stereotypes.
