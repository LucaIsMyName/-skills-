# Social short-form (LinkedIn, Meta, Threads, Bluesky)

**Scope:** Applies to **short social posts and captions** for nonprofit and social-purpose accounts; not long-form articles, press releases, or paid ad policy compliance (follow platform + legal review).

## Excerpt 

- Use for **platform-native** copy: length limits, tone, one main idea per post where possible.
- **Consent** for photos and quotes; **no sensationalizing** beneficiaries or “poverty porn.”
- **Ask** for channel, link URL, campaign name, approval level, and hashtag rules before posting.
- Full file below: per-channel notes, Bad/Good examples, and pre-publish checklist.

## Purpose

This document explains how to write **clear, respectful short-form social content** for **nonprofit and social organizations** on common platforms.

Goals:

- **Reach** supporters without exploiting stories
- **Match** each platform’s norms (length, tone, links)
- **Protect** dignity and consent

## AI / prompt: completeness before drafting

Gather **concrete and meta** inputs. If missing, **ask**—do not invent campaigns, quotes, or image rights.

### Concrete

- **Platform(s)** (LinkedIn, Instagram/Facebook, Threads, Bluesky, other)
- **Draft or final** length limit (characters, caption vs thread)
- **Link** (landing page, donation, event) and **UTM** if required
- **Media:** photo/video subject, **consent on file?** (yes/no/unknown)
- **Quotes:** approved text only; otherwise paraphrase with user confirmation
- **Hashtag list** (brand + campaign) if mandated

### Meta

- **Voice** (warm, professional, activist—within org guidelines)
- **Risk** topics (minors, trauma, legal cases)—may require **no post** until approved
- **Languages** and accessibility (alt text—see [`accessibility-for-comms.md`](accessibility-for-comms.md))

## Core rules

### 1. One main message

Readers skim. Lead with **the action or fact** that matters.

### ❌ Bad

```text
So much is happening at our charity this month and we are so excited to tell you everything!!!
```

### ✅ Good

```text
This week our night shelter adds 15 beds. Volunteers can sign up here: [link]
```

---

### 2. Tone: respectful, not sensational

Avoid pity, “inspiration porn,” or exaggeration.

### ❌ Bad

```text
You won’t BELIEVE what this homeless man did next 💔😱
```

### ✅ Good

```text
People without a safe place to sleep can get a hot meal and a bed here every night. [link]
```

---

### 3. Hashtags and mentions

Use **consistently** per org playbook; don’t spam. On some platforms (e.g. Bluesky), fewer hashtags work better than Instagram.

### ❌ Bad

```text
#charity #love #hope #blessed #give #ngo #help #everyone #viral #fyp
```

### ✅ Good

```text
We’re recruiting peer supporters for our youth mentoring programme. Info: [link] #YouthSupport #OurCity
```

---

### 4. Link placement

Put the **primary link** once, where the platform allows (bio link, first comment, or in-post—follow org rules).

---

### 5. Consent for photos and quotes

- **No** identifiable faces or stories without documented consent (or anonymized copy).
- If consent is unclear: **stop** and ask the communications lead.

---

## Checklist before publish

- [ ] One clear message; key fact or CTA above the fold
- [ ] Tone respectful; no sensational or dehumanizing framing
- [ ] Links and hashtags per org rules; platform length respected
- [ ] Image/video consent confirmed or image not used
- [ ] Quotes approved or clearly generic
- [ ] Alt text planned (see accessibility skill)

---

## Final thought

👉 Short-form social is **public and permanent**. When in doubt, **ask your org lead** before posting sensitive stories.
