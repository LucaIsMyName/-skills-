# Interview preparation

**Scope:** Applies to **preparing a spokesperson to be interviewed**—by journalists, podcast hosts, panel moderators, or a camera. Not a guide to interviewing affected people (see [`writing-a-story.md`](writing-a-story.md) and [`interview-ethics-with-affected-people.md`](interview-ethics-with-affected-people.md) when added). Pair with [`crisis-and-sensitive-topics.md`](crisis-and-sensitive-topics.md) and [`press-statement-basics.md`](press-statement-basics.md).

## Excerpt 

- **Three messages, two stories, one ask.** Go in with this on a card, come out having delivered them.
- **Bridge**, don't dodge. Acknowledge the question, answer what is true, pivot to what matters.
- **Short answers, plain words.** Seven seconds to a minute, not a paragraph.
- **Nothing is off the record** unless agreed in advance in writing. Assume the mic is on.
- Full prep workflow, live tactics, and recovery moves below.

## AI / prompt: before an interview

Before accepting or preparing, clarify **who, what, why, and what success looks like**.

### Concrete

- **Outlet, format, audience** (live radio, recorded TV, news podcast, trade press)?
- **Topic and likely angle**—what has the outlet published recently?
- **Questions known in advance**? Any embargoes or off-limits areas?
- **Duration**, location, live vs. edited, other guests?
- **Goal** of the appearance (inform, defend, invite, recruit)?

### Meta

- Interviews are not conversations; they are **short performances with stakes**.
- Repetition is a feature, not a bug—say the key line more than once, in different words.

## Purpose

Prepare a spokesperson to walk into an interview calm, clear, and with a plan—and to walk out having said what mattered without damage.

---

## 1. Pre-interview workflow

1. **Define the goal**: one sentence, one audience.
2. **Build the message map**: three key messages, two proof points each (story, number, quote).
3. **List the risks**: hostile angles, recent news, internal sore spots, personal history.
4. **Anticipate questions**: friendly, neutral, hostile. Write model answers ≤45 seconds.
5. **Rehearse aloud** at least twice; record one run and listen back.
6. **Brief the host where possible**: background, correct pronunciation, sensitivities.
7. **Logistics**: where, when, what to wear, who is in the room, who is on call.

## 2. Message map (one page)

- **Goal:** _What do we want people to do/know/feel?_
- **Audience:** _Who are we actually talking to?_
- **Message 1:** short statement → proof → bridge back.
- **Message 2:** short statement → proof → bridge back.
- **Message 3:** short statement → proof → bridge back.
- **Call to action / ask:** one concrete next step.

Keep it to one page. If it does not fit, it is not a plan.

## 3. Bridges (acknowledge, answer, pivot)

Useful bridge phrases:

- "What I think matters here is…"
- "The bigger question is…"
- "That connects to something we see every day—…"
- "Let me take a step back. The underlying point is…"

Acknowledge the question so the bridge doesn't feel evasive. If you cannot answer, say **why**, briefly ("I cannot speak to an ongoing investigation, but I can tell you…").

## 4. Dealing with hostile questions

- **Breathe.** One beat before answering is calm, not slow.
- **Correct false premises** early ("That's not the case—let me clarify…"). Do not repeat the false phrase.
- **Don't answer hypotheticals** as if they are real. "If that were true, which it isn't, we would…" is better than speculating.
- **Don't lie and don't guess**. "I don't know, I will follow up" is a fine answer.
- **Silence is okay**. Do not fill every pause.

## 5. Voice and delivery

- Short sentences. Subject-verb-object.
- **One idea per sentence.** Join ideas with a pause, not a comma string.
- Lower the pitch at the end of a statement; a rising pitch sounds tentative.
- Hands visible, still. Eye contact with the interviewer, not the camera—unless it is a direct-to-camera piece.
- On radio and podcast: smile—it is audible.

## 6. Specific formats

### Live TV / radio

- Answer in full sentences so edits aren't needed.
- Assume you will be cut after 20 seconds; the first sentence must carry the message.
- Stay on until released. The mic and camera may stay hot after the "thank you".

### Recorded interview

- It is fine to restart a sentence—"Let me say that again"—and let the editor pick.
- Ask the producer if you can re-phrase a point after the fact.

### Podcast / long form

- The goal is still three messages; you just have more time per bridge.
- Don't get looser with facts because the conversation is friendly.

### Press pack / doorstep

- Have **one prepared line** for the day. Repeat it.
- Do not speculate on the street. "We have a full statement—I'll refer you to our press office."

## 7. What not to do

- **Don't repeat** a hostile phrase ("No, we are not corrupt" becomes the clip).
- **Don't make jokes** on sensitive topics.
- **Don't speak for** people, communities, or coalition partners you haven't asked.
- **Don't go off the record** with a journalist you don't already trust.
- **Don't bash competitors/peers**—the bigger problem is the focus.

## 8. Crisis-specific prep

- Pair with [`crisis-and-sensitive-topics.md`](crisis-and-sensitive-topics.md).
- Know the **lines** agreed with legal (see `crisis-legal-checklist.md` in ethics-safeguarding-legal).
- Care first: "My thoughts are with the people affected."
- Facts: only what is verified.
- Next step: what the org is doing and when the next update is.

## 9. After the interview

- **Thank** the host.
- **Log** what was asked and what you said—future prep material.
- **Review** the broadcast/piece: what landed, what didn't.
- **Correct** public errors via the outlet, not social media fights.

## 10. Interview day checklist

- [ ] One-page message map reread.
- [ ] Goal + one sentence written in the notes.
- [ ] Phone on silent; water nearby; clear desk if remote.
- [ ] Technical check 15 min before start.
- [ ] Press office / comms lead on call for support.

---

## Core idea

A good interview is not **winning**—it is **finishing it with the audience knowing the one thing that matters** and the org's credibility intact. Prepare the plan, say the plan, stay calm.
