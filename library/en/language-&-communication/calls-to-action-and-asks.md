# Calls to action and asks

## Scope:

**CTAs** that invite **specific** next steps—donate, sign, volunteer—without coercion. Not payment processing. Pair with [`fundraising-appeals.md`](fundraising-appeals.md), [`headline-writing.md`](headline-writing.md), [`respectful-language.md`](respectful-language.md), and [`content-design-and-microcopy.md`](../design/content-design-and-microcopy.md).

## Excerpt

- **One primary CTA** per screen/email when possible—**compete** with yourself otherwise.
- **Verb-led** buttons; **outcome**-clear (“Get the guide”, not “Click here”).
- **Match** intensity to **relationship**—cold vs warm audiences.
- **Transparency**: where money goes; **privacy** near forms.
- **Accessibility**: real buttons/links, not mystery meat.

## Before asking

### Concrete

- **What** exactly should happen; **by when**?
- **Friction** you can remove (fields, logins).

### Meta

- **Pressure** tactics spike short-term metrics and **long-term** distrust.

---

## Purpose

Convert **intent** into **action** **ethically**—clear, respectful, **measurable**.

---

## 1. Copy

### Bad: copy

```text
CLICK HERE NOW
```

### Good: copy

```text
Donate £15 to fund one counselling session this week
```

## 2. Page design

- CTA **visible** without scroll on key breakpoints; **repeat** sensibly in long pages.

## 3. What not to do

- **Fake** urgency (countdowns that reset).

---

## Core idea

A CTA is a **clear invitation**, not a **trap**—**specific**, **honest**, **easy**.
