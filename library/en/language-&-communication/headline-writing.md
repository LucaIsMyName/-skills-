# Headline writing

**Scope:** **Headlines** for web, email, social, and print—**clarity**, **accuracy**, **scanning**. Not full SEO engineering. Pair with [`newsletter-and-email.md`](newsletter-and-email.md), [`social-short-form.md`](social-short-form.md), [`respectful-language.md`](respectful-language.md), and [`calls-to-action-and-asks.md`](calls-to-action-and-asks.md).

## Excerpt

- **Promise** only what the body **delivers**—no clickbait cruelty.
- **Front-load** keywords; **one idea** per headline.
- **Tone** matches brand—**calm** beats **hype** for trust orgs.
- **Test** on mobile width—no awkward breaks.
- **Subheads** carry detail—headline stays **short**.

## Before writing headlines

### Concrete

- **Channel** character limits; **preview** snippets.
- **Primary** reader action after click.

### Meta

- Curiosity is fine; **deception** burns trust permanently.

---

## Purpose

Earn **attention** **honestly**—readers know what they get, editors sleep at night.

---

## 1. Patterns

### Bad

```text
You won’t believe what happened next!!!
```

### Good

```text
New evening sessions open at Riverside Youth Hub
```

## 2. Accuracy

- If the story is **nuanced**, say so—**hedge** in the deck, not the facts.

## 3. Inclusive language

- Avoid **othering**; check [`respectful-language.md`](respectful-language.md).

## 4. What not to do

- **Sensationalise** suffering for clicks.

---

## Core idea

Headlines are **promises**—**clear**, **true**, and **kind** to the reader’s time.
