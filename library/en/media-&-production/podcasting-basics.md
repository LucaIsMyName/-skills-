# Podcasting basics

**Scope:** **Audio shows**—format, recording, editing, publishing—at small-org scale. Pair with [`subtitles-and-captions.md`](subtitles-and-captions.md), [`file-naming-and-organising.md`](file-naming-and-organising.md), and [`tone-of-voice-and-brand-voice.md`](../language-&-communication/tone-of-voice-and-brand-voice.md).

## Excerpt

- **Format** decision: interview, narrative, panel—**one** primary style.
- **Room** matters—soft furnishings reduce echo; **test** levels.
- **Host mic** + **guest mic**; **noise reduction** gently—avoid underwater sound.
- **Show notes** with **links** and **timestamps**—accessibility and SEO.
- **Music** licensed; **levels** normalized to platform specs (-16 LUFS approx for stereo podcasts—**verify** host guidance).

## Before episode 1

### Concrete

- **Publishing** host (RSS) and **analytics** plan.
- **Editorial** policy on sensitive topics.

### Meta

- Consistency beats **gear**—**cadence** builds audience.

---

## Purpose

Publish **listenable** audio that **respects** guests and **serves** listeners.

---

## 1. Recording hygiene

### Bad

```text
Two people on one laptop mic.
```

### Good

```text
Separate tracks; room tone; gentle compression in edit—not on noisy source.
```

## 2. What not to do

- **Ambush** guests with off-brief topics without consent.

---

## Core idea

A podcast is **clarity + cadence + care**—**sound** is the product.
