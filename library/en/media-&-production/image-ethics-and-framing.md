# Image ethics and framing

**Scope:** **Ethical** choices in selecting and **cropping** images—stereotypes, dignity, context. Pair with [`photography-for-ngos.md`](photography-for-ngos.md), [`image-and-quote-releases.md`](../ethics-&-legal/image-and-quote-releases.md), and [`respectful-language.md`](../language-&-communication/respectful-language.md).

## Excerpt

- **Framing** controls **power**—what is **centred**, what is **cut off**.
- **Avoid** poverty porn and **saviour** tropes—show **agency** and **systems**.
- **Intersectionality**: **race**, **gender**, **disability**—**stereotypes** harm.
- **Children** and **safeguarding**—extra scrutiny; **no** identifiable images without protocol.
- **Caption** honestly—**when** and **where**; **don’t** invent context.

## Before selecting a hero image

### Concrete

- **Consent** scope for this **use** and **channel**.
- **Alternatives** if the first pick **centres** trauma.

### Meta

- **Empathy** in comms is **shown** by **who** you photograph and **how**.

---

## Purpose

Choose imagery that **respects** subjects and **informs** audiences—**truthfully**.

---

## 1. Bad vs good

### Bad

```text
Anonymous child with dirt smeared for effect.
```

### Good

```text
Named participant (consented), in context, with dignity; caption explains programme support.
```

## 2. What not to do

- **Crop** to remove **watermarks** or **context** that changes meaning.

---

## Core idea

**Framing is argument**—make it **honest**, **consented**, and **just**.
