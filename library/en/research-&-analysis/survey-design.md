# Survey design

## Scope:

**Designing** questionnaires for **surveys** (online or field)—sampling basics, question wording, bias. Not advanced biostatistics. Pair with [`reading-statistics-plainly.md`](reading-statistics-plainly.md), [`notes-and-synthesis.md`](notes-and-synthesis.md), and [`source-evaluation-and-fact-checking.md`](source-evaluation-and-fact-checking.md).

## Excerpt

- Start from the **decision** the survey should inform—**questions follow**.
- **Neutral** wording—avoid leading and double-barrelled questions.
- **Response scales** consistent; **label** endpoints.
- **Pilot** small—fix confusing items early.
- **Ethics**: consent, anonymity limits, safeguarding—especially with vulnerable groups.

## Before fielding a survey

### Concrete

- **Population** and **sampling** approach (even if convenience—**say so**).
- **Length** budget (minutes); device (mobile-friendly).

### Meta

- Bad questions produce **pretty charts** and **wrong** decisions.

---

## Purpose

Collect **actionable**, **ethical** data—so results can be **interpreted** honestly.

---

## 1. Question quality

### Bad: question quality

```text
Don’t you agree our service is excellent?
```

### Good: question quality

```text
How satisfied are you with our service?
(1 very dissatisfied – 5 very satisfied)
```

## 2. Scales

- Odd-number Likert with a **midpoint** unless you have a reason not to.

## 3. Demographics

- Collect **minimum** necessary; **explain** why.

## 4. What not to do

- **Mandatory** sensitive questions without clear need.

---

## Core idea

Surveys are **instruments**—**design**, **pilot**, **document limits**—then interpret with **humility**.
