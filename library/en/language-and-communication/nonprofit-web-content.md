# Nonprofit web content

**Scope:** Applies to **planning, structuring, and writing public website content** for nonprofits and social-sector organisations—page hierarchy, headings, meta descriptions, internal links, and plain-language URLs. Not technical SEO engineering (schema markup, Core Web Vitals tuning in code); pair with [`accessibility-in-code.md`](../coding/accessibility-in-code.md) for developers and [`accessibility-for-comms.md`](accessibility-for-comms.md) for inclusive copy and media.

## Excerpt (read this first)

- **One main job per page.** Don’t overload the homepage with every programme—signpost and link.
- **Headings are an outline.** Use a single logical `<h1>` per page; follow with `<h2>` / `<h3>` in order—helps readers and search engines.
- **Titles and meta descriptions** should match the page; no clickbait; include location or audience if relevant.
- **Internal links** connect related services, reports, and donation paths—reduce orphan pages.
- Full content model, patterns, and checklist below.

## AI / prompt: before writing or restructuring web copy

Clarify **site goals, audience, and what each page must achieve**.

### Concrete

- **Page URL slug** — short, readable, stable; avoid dates in URLs unless necessary.
- **Primary audience** for this page (donor, beneficiary, journalist, partner).
- **Primary action** (donate, apply, contact, download)—one dominant CTA when possible.
- **Legal or policy text** required (privacy, imprint)—do not paraphrase statutory text without review.

### Meta

- Web content is **scanned**, not read like a brochure—front-load meaning in titles and first paragraph.

---

## Purpose

Make your site **easy to understand, easy to navigate, and honest in search results**—without tricks that hurt trust or accessibility.

---

## 1. Information architecture

- **Top tasks first** in navigation: donate, get help, about, contact, key programmes.
- **Avoid deep nests** of “Programmes / Sub / Sub-sub” unless truly needed—flat is often clearer.
- **Breadcrumbs** on deep pages help orientation (design/implementation with your CMS).

---

## 2. Page structure

- **Title tag** — unique per page; include organisation name where helpful.
- **Meta description** — concise summary; not a ranking hack; honest preview in search results.
- **First paragraph** answers: what is this page, for whom, and what to do next.
- **Headings** describe content, not “Section 3” or cute phrases that obscure meaning.

---

## 3. Findability (content SEO)

- Use **words your audience uses** (plain language, including common synonyms).
- **Link related pages** with descriptive anchor text—avoid “click here”.
- **Update or redirect** outdated pages; broken links and stale dates undermine trust.
- If you publish **reports or data**, link from programme pages—helps both users and discovery.

---

## 4. Accessibility of content (not just code)

- **Alt text** for meaningful images; empty alt for decorative—see [`accessibility-for-comms.md`](accessibility-for-comms.md).
- **Readable sentences**; define acronyms on first use on each page (screen-reader users may land mid-page).
- **Forms**: labels, errors, and success messages clear—coordinate with [`forms-and-input-ux.md`](../design/forms-and-input-ux.md).

---

## 5. Trust and compliance

- **Donation pages**: clear use of funds; regulatory statements as required in your jurisdiction.
- **Privacy**: link to full policy near any data collection—see [`data-protection-for-comms.md`](../ethics-safeguarding-legal/data-protection-for-comms.md).

---

## Checklist

- [ ] Each page has a clear purpose and primary CTA (if any).
- [ ] Titles and meta descriptions unique and accurate.
- [ ] Internal links from hub pages to key content.
- [ ] Review for inclusive language and plain definitions.

---

## Core idea

A trustworthy nonprofit site **helps the right people find the right page in one or two clicks**—and tells the truth in the snippet they see in search.
