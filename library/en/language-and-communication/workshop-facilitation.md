# Workshop facilitation

**Scope:** Applies to **planning, running, and following up** workshops and facilitated sessions for teams, communities, or mixed groups in nonprofit and social-sector contexts—training, co-design, retrospectives, focus groups. Not full mediation of legal disputes, not therapy. Pair with [`interviewing-people.md`](../research-and-analysis/interviewing-people.md) for listening skills, [`crisis-and-sensitive-topics.md`](crisis-and-sensitive-topics.md) when topics are heavy, and [`accessibility-for-comms.md`](accessibility-for-comms.md) for inclusive sessions.

## Excerpt (read this first)

- **Outcomes before icebreakers.** Define what participants should leave with; design activities backward from that.
- **Psychological safety** — clear agenda, consent for exercises, opt-out always OK for personal questions.
- **Timeboxing** — fewer objectives done well beat a crowded agenda that runs late.
- **Capture decisions** — who does what by when; share notes while memory is fresh.
- Full flow, inclusion, and checklist below.

## AI / prompt: before designing a workshop

Gather **goal, audience, constraints, and risk level** of the topic.

### Concrete

- **Purpose**: decision, learning, co-design, feedback—pick one primary aim.
- **Participants**: number, roles, languages, accessibility needs, power dynamics.
- **Format**: online, hybrid, in-person; length; breaks; tools (Miro, paper).
- **Sensitive content**: trauma, conflict, minors present—plan safeguards and signposting.

### Meta

- Facilitation is **holding space**, not performing—your job is clarity and fairness, not being the smartest voice.

---

## Purpose

Run sessions where people can **think together productively**, **speak safely**, and **leave with clarity** on next steps.

---

## 1. Design (before the day)

- **Learning or participation outcomes** — write in observable terms (“participants agree on three priorities”).
- **Agenda** with time per block; buffer for questions and tech failure.
- **Materials** accessible in advance where possible (slides, large print, captions).
- **Roles**: facilitator vs. note-taker vs. timekeeper—do not merge if the group is large.

---

## 2. Opening (first 10–15 minutes)

- Welcome, **purpose**, **how the session will run**, **how to participate** (raise hand, chat, etc.).
- **Ground rules**: respect, one conversation at a time, confidentiality as appropriate.
- **Accessibility check**: seating, breaks, captioning, quiet room for online fatigue.

---

## 3. During the session

- **Balance airtime**—invite quieter voices; do not force disclosure.
- **Park off-topic items** on a visible “parking lot” for later.
- **Summarise** decisions and disagreements so the group hears them back fairly.
- **Watch the clock**—negotiate what to drop if behind, rather than silently skipping closure.

---

## 4. Closing

- **Recap decisions and actions** — owner + deadline.
- **Evaluation** — quick (plus/delta, one word, short form).
- **Next steps** — when notes arrive, who follows up.

---

## 5. Aftercare

- Share notes **within 48 hours** when possible.
- **Follow through** on commitments made in the room—or explain why not.

---

## 6. When topics are sensitive

- **Content warnings** where appropriate; offer opt-out from exercises.
- Know **internal escalation** paths if someone discloses risk or harm.
- Do not promise confidentiality you cannot keep (safeguarding overrides).

---

## Checklist

- [ ] Outcomes and agenda aligned; breaks scheduled.
- [ ] Access and language needs addressed.
- [ ] Note-taking and action capture assigned.
- [ ] Safeguarding considered for topic and audience.

---

## Core idea

A good workshop **finishes with shared understanding and named owners**—not just a warm conversation that evaporates.
