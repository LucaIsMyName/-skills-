# Slides and presentations

**Scope:** Applies to **slide decks** (talks, workshops, board packs) for **legibility and structure**; not full motion graphics or video. For **web UI**, use [`designing-good-interfaces.md`](designing-good-interfaces.md).

## Excerpt (read this first)

- **One main idea per slide**; large type; high contrast for projectors.
- **Template discipline**: same margins, title style, and alignment across the deck.
- **Ask** for duration, audience, and whether slides are **speaker notes** or **send-ahead** documents.
- Full file below: rules, Bad/Good examples, checklist.

## Purpose

This document helps **nonprofit and social-sector teams** build presentations that are **easy to follow** and **accessible** in real rooms and on video calls.

## AI / prompt: completeness before drafting

### Concrete

- **Length** (minutes, number of slides)
- **Venue** (projector, laptop-only, hybrid)
- **Brand template** (colors, fonts) if any

### Meta

- **Goal** (inform, persuade, train)
- **Language** level of audience

---

## Core rules

### 1. Legibility at distance

### ❌ Bad

```text
Body text 14pt full paragraphs on a busy background image.
```

### ✅ Good

```text
Title + 3–5 short bullets max; 24pt+ body on slides; plain background.
```

---

### 2. One idea per slide

Split dense content across slides instead of shrinking font.

---

### 3. Contrast

Light text on dark **or** dark text on light; **not** low-contrast brand tints on projectors.

---

### 4. Numbers: one headline number per slide when possible

If comparing metrics, use a **simple chart** or table—avoid walls of numbers.

---

### 5. Motion on slides

**Subtle** transitions; **no** essential content only in fast animation. **Respect** reduced-motion requests in live settings (offer PDF).

---

## Checklist

- [ ] Font sizes safe for **back row** / small video windows
- [ ] Consistent **template** (titles, footers, page numbers if needed)
- [ ] **Speaker notes** hold detail; slides stay scannable
- [ ] **Charts** have titles and units (see [`data-visualization-basics.md`](data-visualization-basics.md))
- [ ] File named and versioned for **handoff** (send-ahead vs live)

---

## Final thought

👉 Slides are **visual cues**, not a document. If everything is on the slide, nothing is.
